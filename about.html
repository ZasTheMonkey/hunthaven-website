Mission statement

To unlock America's private land for the people who love it most.

Wildbook exists to make private hunting land and fishing water accessible to every hunter and angler in America — not just those with the right connections or generational leases. We do this by building the trust, legal framework, and technology that make both sides of the transaction safe, simple, and worth doing.

Vision statement

A future where no good land goes unhunted and no good water goes unfished.

We envision a country where landowners earn meaningful income from their land while hunters and anglers get fair access to private property that's been locked away for decades. Where a first-generation hunter in Florida has the same access as someone whose family has leased land for 40 years. Where the outdoor tradition grows stronger — not because it's exclusive, but because it's open.

What we stand for

Our values

Trust first

Every feature we build starts with one question: does this make the transaction safer for both sides? Verified identities, real insurance, and transparent rules aren't optional — they're the foundation.

Radical transparency

No hidden fees. No buried rules. State regulations, license requirements, bag limits, and the full checkout total are shown before you confirm anything. You should always know exactly what you're getting into.

Both sides win

A marketplace only works when both sides are better off. Landowners should earn real money from land they already own. Guests should get access they couldn't find anywhere else. If one side loses, we all lose.

Respect for the land

We're building for people who take the outdoor tradition seriously. That means enforcing harvest rules, requiring waivers, and giving landowners full control over who accesses their property and under what conditions.

Build lean, ship fast

We're a startup. We move fast, test in the real world, and listen to landowners and hunters before we build features. The best product decisions come from people who are actually out there — not from conference rooms.

Play the long game

Hunting and fishing are traditions that span generations. We're not building for a quick exit. We're building something landowners and hunters will still be using in 20 years — because we did it right.

The problem

64% of US hunters hunt private land. Most got there through who they know.

Private land access in America runs on relationships. If your grandfather didn't lease a piece of ground 30 years ago, you're likely stuck on crowded public land — competing with hundreds of other hunters for the same spots.

On the other side, millions of landowners sit on productive hunting and fishing property that generates zero income. They want to monetize it but don't have a legal, insured, turnkey way to let strangers on their land.

Wildbook closes that gap. We built the infrastructure — insurance, waivers, state regulations, identity verification, and payments — so that connecting hunters to land is as simple as booking an Airbnb.
